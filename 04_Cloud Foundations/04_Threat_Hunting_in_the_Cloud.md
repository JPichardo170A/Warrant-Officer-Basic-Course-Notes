# 🎯 Threat Hunting in the Cloud

*Module 04 — Cloud Foundations | Lesson 04*

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives

- Understand the purpose and methodology of cloud threat hunting
- Apply the "Assume Breach" mindset to cloud environments
- Identify tools and techniques for proactive threat detection

---

## 🧱 What is Cloud Threat Hunting?

Threat hunting is the **proactive search** for indicators of compromise (IOCs), advanced threats, and abnormal behaviors in cloud environments — conducted **before** automated tools raise alerts.

### The Threat Hunting Mindset

> 💡 **Key Takeaway:** "Assume Breach" — actively hunt for subtle traces attackers leave behind rather than waiting for automated alerts.

### Why Hunt in the Cloud?

- Automated detection tools have blind spots
- Advanced persistent threats (APTs) evade signature-based detection
- Cloud environments have unique attack surfaces (API abuse, credential theft, misconfiguration)
- Proactive hunting reduces dwell time

---

## ✅ Summary

- Threat hunting is proactive, not reactive
- The "Assume Breach" mindset drives hunting operations
- Cloud environments require specialized hunting techniques due to unique attack surfaces
- Hunting complements automated detection — it finds what tools miss
